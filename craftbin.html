Craftbin.

A UI focused case study

Problem: Many Parents are collecting a lot of artwork from their children and then sticking them in storage, losing track of their kid's projects or accumulating clutter. A way to digitally save the child's work on their phones in a way that's organized would solve this problem

Solution: CraftBin is a native app for both android and ios. The goal of the app is to allow parents to digitally save their child's arts and crafts. The design also includes a function to allow parents to buy customized gifts with those images. The design was chosen to be native to make it easier for parents to upload pictures directly from their phones.

Overview

How have you met the project objectives?

I designed a native app for both an IOS and an Android Device. I came up with the idea to design an app for parents to be able to save their children's artwork and crafts on a digital portfolio.

What roadblocks did you encounter and how did you overcome them?

I spent a lot of time perfecting the categories screen. I wanted to have pictures as buttons and keep the labels readable. I originally added a small low-opacity white box behind the labels and while it improved readability it didn't look great. I solved the issue by changing the color of the labels, removing the background box and adding a drop shadow to the labels to increase readability. In the end, the labels are easier to read and look nicer and more professional.

If you could, what would you do differently?

The two different versions of my designs look very different to follow the IOS and Material Design guidelines. In the future, if I was designing different versions of the same app I would try to find more common ground between the guidelines to have a more consistent app across platforms.
